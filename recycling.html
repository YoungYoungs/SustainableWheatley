Recycling Guide

We are lucky in Wheatley to be within South Oxfordshire District Council (SODC) who have always taken recycling very seriously and in the latest league tables was joint second among all English local authorities for recycling.

This guide is intended to help residents in Wheatley to re-use and recycle more by

  • Giving greater detail on kerbside bin collections including what can go into each of our wheelie bins (and what can’t)
  • Taking unwanted but useable items to the Wheatley Swapshop
  • Listing charity “bins” (textiles, shoes, clothing etc) in the village
  • Listing nearby Charity shops and other recycling organisations
  • Giving ways to dispose of other items
  • Giving information about our nearest Household Waste Recycling Centre (formerly known as the “dump” or the “tip”) at Redbridge

Oxfordshire County Council have launched Waste Wizard a new initiative to help residents beat the confusion around recycling, reduce waste and so improve recycling rates. In the new online tool, within the OCC website, residents first input their post code and then the name of the item and find out whether it can be repaired, reused, donated, recycled or if it needs to go into a specific bin or can be disposed of at a Waste Recycling Centre. If the item isn’t found, a ‘missing item’ button is also available.

Kerbside Collections

The symbols found on most packaging is informative, but generally cannot be used categorically to decide what can or cannot currently be recycled in the SODC area so if in doubt it’s best to consult the guidance produced by SODC. The Council produces a basic guide which is sent to all households, with a calendar of collections during the year. Some of this information is contained in the pages below. It also produces a very helpful interactive search engine on the SODC Binzone webpage - which you can help update by email or phone if you can’t find your specific query.

On their website you can also find information on collection days, bulky household waste, assisted collections and missed collections.

Also see the lists on the What Can I Recycle? and What Can't be Recycled webpages. You can phone 03000 610610 or email admin.southoxford@biffa.uk if you have a query about any kerbside collection issue.

kerbside wheelie bin guidelines

Collections are usually on MONDAYS (see SODC Collection Days for exceptions) and alternate each week:

Please do bring

  • Unwanted but useable items
  • Mains electrical items, these will be subject to a Portable Appliance Test
  • Items as early as possible and before 10:15am
  • Printer cartridges
  • Foil for recycling like milk bottle tops
  • All items must be given 100% free

Please don't bring

  • Bulky items, a notice board is provided for you to leave a description of your item, a bit like a paper version of Freecycle!
  • Commercial waste
  • Illegal waste
  • Anything dangerous such as knives
  • Anything alive such as pets or livestock
  • Shoes or clothing
  • White goods such as fridges
  • Electric items without a CE marking

You are welcome to only bring items, or to only take things away. Or just come along and browse and have some free refreshments and a chat. This is a social event too, where we all end up with a chance to meet other local families for a Saturday morning chat. Plus we put less rubbish in land-fill, saving both the environment and tax-payers money.

Givers gain space in their homes and the satisfaction of knowing their items are being used elsewhere. You do not need to worry if stuff you bring is not taken while you're there. People may come and take it later, and anything left over will be disposed of through Freecycle, charity shops or recycling, as appropriate.
